\subsection{Part 1: Thermal Neutron Detection}
\FloatBarrier
We measured 10-second counts with detector biases at 100~V increments from 0~V to 1800~V, which can be observed for each of these counts in \figref{counting_curve}.
We ceased counts after 1800~V to avoid potential detector damage, as by this voltage the count rate began rapidly increasing at increased bias.
Based on the plateau in count rates between 1200~V and 1600~V, we selected an operating voltage of 1400~V, to minimize potential variation in sensitivity.\\

We then successfully measured a spectrum of counts over 10~minutes for the \PuBe source and \HeThree detector, which may be viewed in \figref{exp1_spectrum}, in which we label the energy peaks corresponding to the energies of tritium and proton products, alongside the sum peak.

\defplot{exp1_spectrum}{The energy spectrum measured over 10~minutes for the \PuBe source (as thermalized through polyethylene) and \HeThree detector. The energy peaks corresponding to neutron-\HeThree reaction products (proton, tritium, and coincidence) are identified and labeled.}

\subsection{Part 2: Fast Neutron Detection}
\FloatBarrier
We measured energy spectra of the \NaSrc and \CsSrc sources, plotted in \figref{fig:nacs-calib}.
Knowing the Compton edge energies for the 661.7~keV \CsSrc peak (477~keV) and the 511~keV and 1.2~MeV \NaSrc peaks (341~keV and 989~keV, respectively), we observed a linear relationship between event energy and the channel of detector response, seen in \figref{cal_three}.
Using this relationship, we estimated a linear calibration relationship of $E=1.855c + 101.067$~keV (where $E$ is event energy and $c$ the corresponding channel on the spectrum), and used this to calibrate further spectra.
